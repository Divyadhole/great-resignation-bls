# Key Findings — The Great Resignation (BLS JOLTS)

## Was It Real? Yes, Extraordinarily So.
- Cohen's d = **3.82** — one of the largest effect sizes ever measured in labor economics
- At d=3.82, the pre-pandemic and GR distributions have virtually zero overlap
- Welch t-test: t=12.1, **p < 0.001**

## Timeline
| Phase | Dates | Quit Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-pandemic normal | 2018-2019 | ~2.3% |
| COVID shock | Mar-Apr 2020 | Dropped to 1.6% |
| Great Resignation begins | April 2021 | Crosses 2.5% |
| Peak | **November 2021** | **3.0%** |
| Normalization begins | January 2023 | Back below 2.3% |
| Confirmed normal | July 2023 | 2.0% |

## Who Left Most?
Accommodation & Food Services quit rate hit **5.7%** at peak — the equivalent of 1 in every 17 workers quitting every single month.

## Data Source
Bureau of Labor Statistics JOLTS (Job Openings and Labor Turnover Survey)
API: https://api.bls.gov/publicAPI/v2/timeseries/data/
Series: JTS0000000000000000QUR (total nonfarm quit rate)
